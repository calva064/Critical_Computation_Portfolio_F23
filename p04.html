Exquisite Corpse

Description

My primary goal this week was to push myself outside of my comfort zone. Taking on board Regina's feedback from last week that the sketches I was producing were cartoonish and lacked variety, I wanted to try making something different from my previous three sketches. I ensured to incorporate the required technical components into my sketch, user-defined functions; arguments; and parameters, but beyond this I gave myself creative freedom to explore and experiment. Please note that you may have to play the sketch directly from my p5.js page rather than my Glitch portfolio, as I can't seem to hear the sound when I play the sketch from Glitch.

Design Process and Reflection

Ultimately, I decided not to draw a human or non-human figure using the sketches me and my partners, Arden and Prashant, had drawn. I truly wanted to give myself creative liberty to make something different this week and feel comfortable with creating from a place of wanting to make something cool rather than restricting myself to the confines of an assignemnt. Since I'm passionate about music, I decided I wanted to design either an album cover or a website for a musician. I looked at some musicians' websites for inspiration, and I also looked at album covers. Noticing that many musicians feature photos on the covers of their albums, I was inspired to use images for my sketch. I also wanted to incorporate text into my sketch, since I haven't used text for the past three assignments.

Since I sought to experiment with this sketch, I struggled to create an initial hand-drawn sketch of what I planned to execute in p5.js; I knew I would be spending most of my time experimenting and playing around in p5.js in order to get a sense for how I could develop my aesthetic. I did some basic mind-mapping, and here is an image of notes from my brainstorming session:

As the image above shows, some initial ideas I had were to: outline a woman's body using squiggles, pull music into p5.js, fade in/out elements, and incorporate music into my design that syncs up with animation. I ultimately decided that I wanted to design some kind of kind of visual for a musician's website incorporating these ideas.

I began by loading album artwork from a musician I like, Rhye, into p5.js and experimenting with how the image was rendered. I added a tint to the image and then began thinking about how I could incorporate text into my design. Since my sketch revolves around music, I wanted to incorporate his song lyrics into my design. I thought that using song lyrics to outline or draw the woman in the image, in order to create my human form, could be visually exciting.

In order to know where to place the lyrics along the woman's body, I found a tutorial online that demonstrated how to trace an image in p5.js by printing the mouse's X and Y coordinates to the console: console.log(mouseX,mouseY). I used this to get precise coordinates for my lyrics so that I was able to place them precisely along the curvature of her body.

While making this sketch, I learned how to load font into p5.js, create a tint on an image, and create a gradient with color. I also reviewed several Coding Train videos to remind myself of how Noise works, as I wanted to use Noise in my sketch. While I considered incorporating gradients and Noise into my sketch, I ultimately didn't like how my sketch looked when I did this. Here are some screenshots of my work in progress incorporating gradients:

Taking inspiration from a collage I saw recently, I realized that my sketch would look better if I blacked out the background and part of the woman's arm so that the sketch looked more like a collage than an image!

After implementing this change, I turned my attention back to the text. I was interested in making the text's color and size a function of time. I wanted to load Rhye's music into p5.js and have the text fade/appear and get bigger/smaller depending on where in the song the music was playing. I ran into some hurdles while trying to do this. For one, I could not determine how to load the entire sound file into p5.js. p5.js said the file size was too big, so I ended up recording the audio on my phone and then loading that file into p5.js. Next, I took advantage of the millis() function in order to control when the lyrics appeared on the screen so that they timed up with the music.

Overall, this week was a huge challenge for me. I threw myself into the deep-end, not letting myself rely on what I know and am comfortable with: creating cartoonish characters out of 2D primitives. As a result, the end product of my experimentations is not as well-developed as I had envisioned, and I would love to continue working on this sketch so that I can get the fade in/fade out part of my code to function better, since I think this would look great. Since I made a lot of adjustments to my code, this affected the fade in/fade out lines I wrote in my code. I do like how my sketch looks aesthetically, though.
